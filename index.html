    • What is Asset Recovery?

    • We Are Here to Help

    Asset recovery refers to the process of reclaiming funds, assets, or properties that rightfully belong to an individual or entity.

    It often involves identifying and securing resources that may have been overlooked or lost due to various circumstances, such as tax deed overages, foreclosure, or other financial transactions.

    • In the context of our services, asset recovery includes excess proceeds and surplus funds recovered from tax deed auctions, mortgage overages statewide, property tax refunds, and many more.
    • When a property is sold at auction for an amount exceeding the outstanding tax or debt, the excess funds are held in reserve until the former owner claims those funds. Sometimes, property owners are owed property tax refunds when the value of the property is reassessed for a surplus by the County Accessor. Often, there is a deadline to claim them.
    • Our asset recovery professionals work diligently to locate these surplus funds and assist rightful owners in claiming what is rightfully theirs. This includes heirs to individuals that may be deceased (children, siblings, etc). It also includes managers or officers of active, merged, or suspended businesses and companies (Corporation, LLC, LP, General Partnership, Sole Proprietor, etc) .
